Federal Go-to-Market for Startups

We help pre-seed to Series A startups win their first government customers and build the company to support them.

Common Client Situations

“Our product has obvious government application but we do not know how to get in front of the right people.”
“We have early government conversations but nothing converts to pilots or contracts.”

How Acuity Helps

We build the essentials a young company needs to sell to the U.S. government: go-to-market strategy grounded in how agencies actually buy, capture and pipeline discipline, compliance foundations, administrative and back-office setup, key hires including cleared talent, client-facing collateral, product strategy shaped to government use cases, and a partnership program that extends reach without giving away the deal.

Typical Deliverables

  • Government-facing collateral and capability sheets
  • Product strategy for government use cases
  • Partnership and teaming program design
  • Hiring plan for cleared and government-facing talent

Best-Fit Clients

Who We Are

Brad Belzak

CEO and Founder

Acuity Global Partners is led by founder Brad Belzak, a senior executive and advisor with 24 years across government, defense, technology, consulting, finance, and global risk — supported by vetted specialists in law, tax, security, investigations, and local-market advisory.

His career has moved between public service and the private sector for two decades. He began at the Department of Homeland Security in its earliest days, holding progressive roles across intelligence, disaster response, and policy — FEMA during Hurricane Katrina, the FBI Terrorism Screening Center, and aviation security at TSA — before helping write aviation-security legislation on the U.S. Senate Homeland Security Committee. He then advised governments and multinationals through crisis and growth at Deloitte, Crumpton Group, and EY, from the 2011 Thai floods and post-conflict Libya to risk and investigations across Europe, the Middle East, and Southeast Asia.

He returned to government to direct homeland defense integration at the Pentagon, where he helped lead the U.S. response to the Chinese spy balloon incident, then served as second-in-command of the Export-Import Bank and helped build the public sector practice at Strider Technologies. He currently serves as a Commissioner on the Washington Metrorail Safety Commission, holds degrees from Northeastern and Elon, is a Certified Fraud Examiner, and maintains an active Top Secret / SCI clearance.
