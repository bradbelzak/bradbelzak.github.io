What Acuity Does

A boutique advisory firm for leaders operating across borders, risk, and complexity.

We help clients evaluate and enter new markets, structure operations abroad, assess political and regulatory risk, vet partners and counterparties, navigate U.S. and international government environments, and respond when conditions change.

Our clients make decisions where the cost of being wrong is high. They operate in markets where relationships, context, and judgment matter as much as data. They need an advisor who has run operations inside the machine, not just advised from outside it.

We work directly with clients. Every engagement is led by Brad Belzak, a Washington, DC-based senior executive with 24 years across federal government, Big Four consulting, and technology. We bring in vetted specialists in law, tax, security, investigations, and local-market advisory where the work requires them.

1. Federal Go-to-Market for Startups

We help pre-seed to Series A startups win their first government customers and build the company to support them.

Common Client Situations

  • “Our product has obvious government application but we do not know how to get in front of the right people.”
  • “We have early government conversations but nothing converts to pilots or contracts.”
  • “We have no compliance, contracting, or back-office infrastructure for federal work.”
  • “We need government-ready collateral, pricing, and a partnership strategy before our next raise.”

How Acuity Helps

We build the essentials a young company needs to sell to the U.S. government: go-to-market strategy grounded in how agencies actually buy, capture and pipeline discipline, compliance foundations, administrative and back-office setup, key hires including cleared talent, client-facing collateral, product strategy shaped to government use cases, and a partnership program that extends reach without giving away the deal.

Typical Deliverables

  • Federal go-to-market strategy and capture plan
  • Compliance and back-office readiness roadmap
  • Government-facing collateral and capability sheets
  • Product strategy for government use cases
  • Partnership and teaming program design
  • Hiring plan for cleared and government-facing talent

Best-Fit Clients

Pre-seed to Series A founders with government-applicable products, venture-backed teams making their first federal hires, and investors preparing portfolio companies for public-sector revenue.

2. National Security Technology Advisory

Senior counsel on product, policy, and positioning for companies operating in defense, intelligence, and homeland security markets.

Common Client Situations

  • “Policy and budget currents are shifting and we need to know where the mission is moving before our competitors do.”
  • “We are a growth-stage or non-U.S. firm and need to position credibly in national security markets.”
  • “Our board and investors need an independent read on our government strategy and exposure.”
  • “We need partnerships and channels that create leverage, not dependency.”

How Acuity Helps

We advise boards, executives, and investors on where mission demand is heading, how policy and budget dynamics shape it, how to position and message for government audiences, and how to build partnership and channel architecture that compounds. This counsel draws on Pentagon policy leadership, senior roles at DHS and the Senate, and building the civilian public-sector division at a national security technology company.

Typical Deliverables

  • Market and mission landscape assessment
  • Positioning and messaging for government audiences
  • Partnership and channel architecture
  • Board and investor briefings on government strategy and risk
  • Allied and adjacent market expansion strategy

Best-Fit Clients

Growth-stage national security and dual-use technology companies, defense-adjacent firms expanding into civilian or allied markets, and investors evaluating national security technology.

3. Fractional Chief of Staff & Private Client Advisory

A senior operating partner for principals, family offices, and founders, at home and abroad, without the full-time hire.

Common Client Situations

  • “I need senior execution capacity I can trust, but not a full-time hire.”
  • “My advisors each see one slice of my affairs; no one owns the whole picture.”
  • “I have a sensitive project, transaction, or overseas move that needs to be run quietly and well.”
  • “Something just went wrong and I need someone who has managed a crisis before.”

How Acuity Helps

We run the principal’s operating rhythm wherever the principal needs it, in the U.S. or overseas: decision cadence, priority execution, and preparation for high-stakes meetings. We coordinate and hold accountable the full advisor bench across legal, tax, wealth, and security so the principal gets one integrated picture. We vet counterparties before commitment, manage sensitive projects end to end, and serve as crisis quarterback when conditions change. Judgment drawn from 24 years across the U.S. government and Big Four, including service as second in command of a 550-person federal agency and Chief of Staff to EY’s Global Forensics Leader. Discretion is standard; scope is explicit.

Typical Deliverables

  • Principal operating cadence and priority management
  • Advisor coordination and accountability structure
  • Counterparty and opportunity vetting
  • Sensitive project management, U.S. and international
  • Crisis decision support and escalation protocols

Best-Fit Clients

High-net-worth principals and family offices, founders and executives between senior hires, and private clients with cross-border interests.

4. Global Market Entry & Complex Environment Advisory

Where to enter, how to structure, whom to trust, and how to operate, from stable markets to fragile and conflict-adjacent environments.

Common Client Situations

  • “We see an opportunity in a country or region but cannot assess whether it is real, who to trust, or how to execute.”
  • “We need to stand up operations abroad, in a developed market or a hard one, quickly and correctly.”
  • “Political conditions in our operating market are deteriorating and we need to understand our exposure.”
  • “We are evaluating a presence in a fragile or conflict-adjacent market and need an honest assessment, not a sales pitch.”

How Acuity Helps

We conduct market-entry and operating assessments that go beyond publicly available data: political and regulatory risk, stakeholder mapping, local partner vetting, operating model design, and escalation frameworks. We stand up operations on short notice in developed markets and hard places alike: a European capital this quarter, a conflict-adjacent environment in the Middle East the next. Where licensed services are required, we coordinate with qualified partners and make that structure transparent. We also advise on U.S. export finance options, drawn from leadership of a $135 billion portfolio at EXIM Bank.

Typical Deliverables

  • Country and market-entry risk assessment
  • Operating model design and launch plan
  • Stakeholder and relationship mapping
  • Local partner, vendor, and advisor vetting
  • Escalation, contingency, and withdrawal planning
  • Export finance advisory

Best-Fit Clients

Multinationals, family offices with international assets, investors assessing country risk, NGOs and international organizations, and private clients with presence in sensitive markets.

Regions supported: Europe · Middle East · Latin America · Southeast Asia
